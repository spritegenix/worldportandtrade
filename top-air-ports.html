Skytrax’s Top 7 best airports in the World 2024

Home > Top Airports

These airports set the standard for air travel excellence from Asia to Europe to the Middle East. Here are the top 7 best airports in the world according to Skytrax:

1. Changi Airport, Singapore (SIN)

So, what sets Changi Airport apart from the rest? First and foremost, Changi Airport is known for its stunning architecture and design. The airport features various indoor gardens and waterfalls, including the world’s tallest waterfall at the Jewel Changi Airport.
The airport also boasts an impressive collection of art installations and sculptures, making it a true feast for the senses.
In addition to its aesthetic appeal, this world-class airport is known for its outstanding amenities and services. The airport offers various shopping and dining options, including a 24-hour food court serving delicious local cuisine.
Changi Airport also features a rooftop swimming pool, movie theaters, and a butterfly garden where visitors can relax and unwind before or after their flights.
They also use a QR code loyalty program to reward their passengers. An effective marketing strategy keeps customers happy and satisfied with their services.
Another reason making it the best airport in the world is its efficiency and convenience. The airport is well-organized and easy to navigate, with clear signages and helpful staff ready to assist passengers.
Changi Airport also offers free Wi-Fi, making it easy for travelers to stay connected and get work done while on the go.

2. Changi Airport, Singapore (SIN)

The airport offers luxurious lounges, including the Al Mourjan Business Lounge and the exclusive Al Safwa First Lounge, which provide travelers with a tranquil and comfortable space to relax and unwind before their flights. These lounges offer a range of airport amenities that set them apart from other airport lounges, such as gourmet dining options, spa treatments, and private sleeping rooms.
Another unique feature of Hamad International Airport is its art collection. The airport is home to over 20 permanent art installations and sculptures, as well as rotating exhibitions that showcase the work of local and international artists.
These artworks add a touch of elegance and sophistication to the airport, making it a true cultural destination for travelers.
It also has innovative technology and cutting-edge facilities. It boasts an advanced transit system that connects passengers between terminals in just a few minutes, making it easy and convenient for travelers to navigate the airport.
It also features a range of technological advancements, such as automated immigration gates and self-service check-in kiosks, that streamline the experience and reduce wait times.

3. Tokyo International Airport (HND)

One of the standout features of Haneda Airport is its state-of-the-art terminal buildings. They provide self-check-in kiosks and automated baggage handling systems, which help reduce wait times and streamline the passenger experience. The airport also offers a range of dining and shopping options and amenities like lounges, showers, and even a traditional Japanese bathhouse.
Another reason Haneda Airport is worthy of being among the best airports in the world is its commitment to safety and security.
The airport has implemented a range of measures to ensure the safety and comfort of passengers, including advanced security screening systems and an on-site medical center.
And on top of all its features and amenities, Haneda Airport is only 14 kilometers from downtown Tokyo, making it a popular choice for travelers who want to explore the city before or after their flight.

4. Incheon International Airport (ICN)

Incheon International Airport is another busy airport serving as a central hub for East Asia.
The airport was designed with passenger comfort and convenience in mind, featuring spacious terminals with plenty of natural light and modern amenities such as free Wi-Fi, charging stations, and comfortable seating areas.
They also boast a range of shopping and dining options, from luxury boutiques to casual cafes and restaurants. It also has various services and facilities to ensure passengers’ smooth and comfortable travel experiences.
It includes a range of lounges for business and first-class passengers, baggage storage and delivery, and even a transit hotel for those with extended layovers.
Additionally, the airport strongly focuses on accessibility, with wheelchair-friendly facilities and services available.
Incheon International Airport is also known for its commitment to safety and security. The airport has implemented advanced security measures and screening technologies to ensure the safety and comfort of all passengers.
The airport also has a medical center with trained medical staff available 24/7 to assist with any medical emergencies that may arise.

5. Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG)

Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport, also known as Roissy Airport, is the largest international airport in France and is among the busiest airports in Europe.
The airport has recently undergone significant renovations and expansions, with new terminals and upgraded passenger amenities.
The airport features a wide range of shopping and dining options, from luxury boutiques to fast-food restaurants, as well as comfortable lounges for premium passengers.
One key advantage of the airport is its efficiency and connectivity. The airport is well-connected to Paris and other major European cities, with various transport options, including trains, buses, and taxis.
The airport also has advanced technology and equipment to facilitate smooth and efficient operations, such as self-check-in kiosks, automated baggage handling systems, and state-of-the-art security screening.

6. Istanbul Airport (IST)

Located in Turkey, Istanbul Airport is one of the newest airports in the world, having opened in 2018. Despite being new in the game, Istanbul Airport has quickly made a name for itself as one of the best airports in the world.
One key factor that sets Istanbul Airport apart is its massive area of 76.5 million square meters. The airport can accommodate more than 200 million passengers per year, making it one of the largest airports in the world.
But despite its size, the airport is easy to navigate, thanks to its intuitive layout and wayfinding tools.
Istanbul Airport is also known for its excellent customer service and hospitality. The airport staff and employees are friendly, helpful, and welcoming, making passengers feel at ease when they arrive.
The airport also has a wide range of options for shopping and dining. It also hosts various amenities, including a mosque, a hotel, and lounges for all travelers.

7. Munich Airport (MUC)

Thanks to its innovative design, top-notch facilities, and excellent customer service, it has consistently ranked among the best airports in the world. One of the features of Munich Airport is its unique Terminal 2, which provides a seamless passenger experience. It has cutting-edge technology, such as automated check-in and security processes, that lessen wait times and smoothens the travel process.
The airport also offers a range of dining and shopping options and amenities like lounges, spas, and play areas for children.
It commits itself to sustainability and has implemented various environmentally-friendly initiatives, such as using renewable energy sources, reducing waste and emissions, and implementing water conservation measures.
This green initiative has earned Munich Airport multiple accolades and recognition, including being named the world’s first airport to achieve carbon-neutral status.
